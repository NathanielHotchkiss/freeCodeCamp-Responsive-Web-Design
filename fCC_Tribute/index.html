Elon Musk

Founder, CEO, lead designer of SpaceX
CEO, product architect of Tesla, Inc.
Founder of The Boring Company and X.com
Co-founder of Neuralink, OpenAI, and Zip2
Chairman of SolarCity

Portrait of Elon Musk

Elon Reeve Musk is an American entrepreneur who got his start with Zip2 and X.com (an online bank which later became Paypal). With the money Elon made from the sale of Paypal to Ebay, he founded a company known as SpaceX in 2002. He dreamt of the affordable colonization of Mars, and the company has gone on to revolutionize space travel. SpaceX created reusable rockets which drastically cut down on the cost of launching a rocket. They created the Falcon Heavy, which is double the world's most powerful rocket. Arguably their greatest achievement is being the first private spacecraft to send astronauts to the ISS. The rocket that carried the astronauts used advanced AI to automatically dock. In 2004, he started to invest in Tesla, an electric car company co-founded by Martin Eberhard. Musk not only invested in Tesla, he actually helped develop its first car, the Roadster, and not long after he became CEO. Once again, he helped revolutionize a market. Musk knew that electric cars were the future, and he knew he had to create an electric car the average person would be interested in, now it seems like every car manufacturer is scrambling to compete. Outside of SpaceX and Tesla, Elon has gone on to form other companies and ideas, like The Boring Company, Solar City, Neuralink, and OpenAI.
